Primary target level: SCI Q4-oriented, with Q3 only if the editor values the external-validation and transportability audit angle.

Submission message:

This is not a high-performance diagnostic model paper. It is a cross-cohort validation and transportability audit of public Alzheimer's disease blood transcriptomic signatures.

Required final check before submission:

- Confirm current SCIE/JCR inclusion and quartile in the 2026 Journal Citation Reports or institutional Web of Science access.
- Confirm APC, article type, word limit, figure/table limit, and whether public-data bioinformatics studies are accepted.
- Check recent 2024-2026 articles in each candidate journal for public transcriptomics, external validation, and negative/limited-transferability studies.

## Recommended Submission Sequence

### 1. BMC Medical Genomics

Priority: primary.

Rationale:
The official scope covers genomic research related to human health and disease, including neurogenomics, clinical genomics, and novel bioinformatics methods on medical data.

Fit:
Strongest if the paper emphasizes medical-genomics validation standards, neurogenomics context, public-data reproducibility, and limitations of diagnostic transferability.

Risk:
May require clearer novelty than a standard public-data reanalysis.

Action before submission:
Strengthen Methods and Discussion around pre-specified validation, leakage control, and transportability audit.

### 2. BioData Mining

Priority: primary/backup.

Rationale:
The official scope covers data mining and machine-learning applications to high-dimensional biological and biomedical data, including preprocessing, modeling, interpretation, and knowledge discovery.

Fit:
Good if framed as a model-transportability and validation-failure audit rather than as an AD biomarker discovery paper.

Risk:
May expect computational-method novelty. The current manuscript has stronger evaluation rigor than algorithmic novelty.

Action before submission:
Emphasize reproducible validation workflow, cross-platform degradation metrics, and claim-audit framework.

### 3. BMC Genomics

Priority: ambitious backup.

Rationale:
The official scope covers transcriptomics and broader genomics, and BMC Series journals judge manuscripts by scientific validity rather than perceived impact.

Fit:
Possible if the manuscript is presented as a transcriptomic reproducibility and validation study.

Risk:
Likely higher novelty requirement. The pathway result is conservative and may not be enough for editorial enthusiasm.

Action before submission:
Use only if the final draft is polished, figures are strong, and the methodological validation angle is clear.

### 4. Medicine

Priority: Q4-oriented broad backup.

Rationale:
Wolters Kluwer lists Medicine as a fully open-access broad medical journal indexed in Web of Science / Journal Citation Reports.

Fit:
Possible if the article is framed as a biomedical validation study relevant to AD diagnostic research.

Risk:
Broad scope can be permissive, but pure bioinformatics without clinical data may be reviewed unevenly.

Action before submission:
Make the abstract and introduction clinically understandable, keep limitations prominent, and avoid technical overloading.

### 5. Genetics Research

Priority: backup with indexing caution.

Rationale:
The Cambridge page describes scope including genomics, bioinformatics, computational studies, and disease genetics, but also notes publisher transition after 2020.

Fit:
Topic fit may be acceptable.

Risk:
Current publisher/indexing status must be verified carefully. Do not use as primary until JCR/SCIE status is confirmed.

### 6. BMC Research Notes

Priority: non-primary fallback.

Rationale:
The official scope explicitly welcomes valid negative results and short research observations.

Fit:
Good fallback if the project is reduced to a short validation note.

Risk:
May not satisfy the SCI Q3/Q4 objective. Use only if full-article submissions fail or if target is changed.

### Excluded for Current Manuscript: Molecular Biology Reports

Reason:
The official scope states that manuscripts need molecular or cell biology techniques and that pure bioinformatic / in silico papers are not published. Current Route B has no wet-lab validation.

## Submission Package Requirements

Files needed before first submission:

- Main manuscript in DOCX or journal-compatible LaTeX/Word.
- Four main figures from B4, polished and relabeled.
- Supplementary tables containing full DEG, validation, model, stable/unstable, and enrichment outputs.
- Data availability statement listing GEO accessions and local reproducibility scripts.
- Code availability statement pointing to a repository or compressed code supplement.
- Ethics statement: public de-identified GEO datasets, no new human-subject recruitment.
- Limitations paragraph must include cross-platform failure, no wet-lab validation, and blood-cell composition uncertainty.

## Cover Letter Core Message

Suggested cover letter paragraph:

We submit an original bioinformatics validation study evaluating the cross-cohort transportability of Alzheimer's disease peripheral blood transcriptomic signatures. Rather than presenting a high-performance diagnostic classifier, the manuscript provides a staged external-validation audit across related and independent cohorts, showing strong related-cohort reproducibility but marked cross-platform diagnostic degradation. We believe this cautious result is relevant to genomic medicine and bioinformatics readers because it clarifies the evidentiary threshold required before public blood transcriptomic signatures are interpreted as diagnostic biomarkers.

## Rejection Risk and Response

Risk 1: "Insufficient novelty."

Response:
Emphasize pre-specified multi-cohort validation, leakage control, failed diagnostic transferability as the central finding, and reproducible claim-audit workflow.

Risk 2: "No wet-lab validation."

Response:
Position as public-data validation and transportability audit; do not claim mechanism or clinical utility.

Risk 3: "Pathway findings are housekeeping-like."

Response:
State explicitly that stable genes show conservative translation/RNA-processing context, not AD-specific causal mechanisms.

Risk 4: "Classifier performance is weak."

Response:
This is the main finding, not a weakness hidden in the supplement. The manuscript is about limited transferability.
